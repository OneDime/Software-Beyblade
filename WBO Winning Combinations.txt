This sheet summarizes combo and part performance data pulled from the WBO Winners Thread, a public record of podium-finishing combos from WBO-sanctioned Beyblade X tournaments.

Each combo or part is assigned “Competitive Points” based on how often it appears on the podium and how recently it won. More recent results are weighted more heavily.														
Competitive Points = Frequency × Recency Weight (recency weight only applied to the full dataset, not monthly datasets)													
Wins from the past 30 days = ×10													
Wins from 31–90 days ago = ×3													
Wins from 91–180 days ago = ×1													
Wins 181+ days ago = ×0.25													
The “Sample Size (n)” column shows how many times that combo or part appeared in the dataset — giving context to the points total.

Note: For CX Lockchip performance, I've removed all lockchips that are not weighted in order for blade performance to be captured entirely in one row. So if Dran Brave Slash 3-60 Low Rush has 1 point, and Pegasus Brave Slash 3-60 Low Rush has 1 point, the data is cleaned so it just shows Brave Slash 3-60 Low Rush with 2 points. Combos that utilize a weighted lockchip (Valkyrie, Emperor, etc.) are in their own category, so in this example, Valkyrie Brave Slash 3-60 Low Rush would NOT be bundled with Brave Slash 3-60 Low Rush.												
Data is broken down across different views:														
Full combo rankings													
Combo rank changes vs. previous month													
Individual part rankings (Blade, Ratchet, Bit, Assist Blade)													
Individual sheets for specific time ranges (e.g., June 2025 only)													


This is not opinion-based or tier list speculation — it’s a data-driven snapshot of what has actually performed in tournaments.														
The tool used to generate this data is custom-coded and not yet public, but this sheet contains all the output data you need to explore trends, optimize builds, and track performance.
